Graduate Employability Is a European Problem

From Dublin to Berlin, universities face the same pressure: prove that degrees lead to employment. The numbers are getting harder to ignore.

14.5%

EU Youth Unemployment

Eurozone youth unemployment remains stubbornly high. In Ireland, 10.8% of under-25 graduates are unemployed. Universities are judged on what happens after graduation.

€4bn+

DFHERIS Budget Under Pressure

Ireland's Department of Further and Higher Education invests over €4bn annually. The €307M funding gap (vs OECD average) demands proof of outcomes, not just enrolment.

82%

IBEC surveys show 82% of Irish employers report graduate skills gaps. CVs and transcripts don't tell employers what a graduate can actually do. The trust gap costs everyone.
